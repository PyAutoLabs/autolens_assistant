# Sources: mass models

Papers on the mass-model parametrisations used in strong lensing.

## Shajib 2020 — Gaussian decomposition

**Reference:** Shajib 2020 — Gaussian decomposition
**Concepts:** [[mass-models]] (MGE)
**Summary:** Decomposes Sérsic / generalised-NFW profiles into
sums of Gaussians for fast analytic deflection. Widely adopted for
flexible mass models that follow stellar light.

## Oguri 2021 — Cored Steep Ellipsoid (CSE)

**Reference:** Oguri 2021 — Cored Steep Ellipsoid (CSE)
**Concepts:** [[mass-models]]
**Summary:** Introduces an MGE-like expansion in cored steep
ellipsoids — analytic deflection, flexible angular structure. Used in
glafic and other codes.

## He 2024 — MGE

**Reference:** He 2024 — MGE
**Concepts:** [[mass-models]], [[bulge-halo-decomposition]]
**Summary:** MGE-based lens modelling applied to a
specific science target.

## Shajib 2021 — mass slope of NFW

**Reference:** Shajib 2021 — mass slope of NFW
**Concepts:** [[mass-models]], [[radial-angular-degeneracy]]
**Summary:** Uses a SLACS-like sample to show that lensing-only
constraints on the total mass-density slope are consistent with joint
lensing+dynamics analyses, and that the dark-matter haloes of massive
ellipticals are on average compatible with an uncontracted NFW profile.

## Shajib 2019 — is every strong lens happy

**Reference:** Shajib 2019 — is every strong lens happy
**Concepts:** [[mass-models]]
**Summary:** Uniformly models a sample of 13 quadruply imaged quasar
lenses, showing that different systems fail in different ways under
overly simple macromodel assumptions and motivating more flexible,
system-by-system model comparison.

## Tian — Einstein rings mass discrepancy

**Reference:** Tian — Einstein rings mass discrepancy
**Concepts:** [[einstein-radius]], [[radial-angular-degeneracy]]
**Summary:** Examines discrepancies between masses inferred from
Einstein radii and those inferred from other tracers, using ring-based
lensing masses as a population-level cross-check on simplified dynamical
or isothermal estimates.

## Li 2018 — SIS / Einstein-mass discrepancy

**Reference:** Li 2018 — SIS / Einstein-mass discrepancy
**Concepts:** [[einstein-radius]], [[mass-models]]
**Summary:** Apparent discrepancy between SIS-based Einstein
mass and alternative estimates.

## Bolton 2008 — SLACS V / full ACS strong-lens sample

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Bolton2008`
**Reference:** ApJ 682, 964--984; arXiv:0805.1931; doi:10.1086/589327
**Concepts:** [[slacs]], [[einstein-radius]], [[mass-models]]

**Supports:**
- The full SLACS ACS sample contains 63 clear galaxy-scale strong lenses
  with singular isothermal ellipsoid and light-traces-mass models fitted
  to the imaging data.
- The paper supplements the strong-lensing mass measurements with
  magnitudes, effective radii, redshifts, and velocity dispersions, making
  it a standard benchmark for galaxy-scale mass measurements.

**Use when:**
- Citing SLACS as a reference sample for strong-lensing mass
  measurements or for the relationship between mass, light, and
  kinematics in early-type galaxies.

**Do not use for:**
- Euclid Q1 catalogue statistics or JWST lens-finding performance.
