# Microlensing-IMF

## TL;DR

Stars in the lens galaxy microlens images of lensed quasars. The
microlensing variability amplitude depends on the **surface density of
compact objects** along the line of sight to each image. Comparing that
to the total surface density (from the macro lens model) gives the
stellar mass fraction near each image — i.e. the [[mass-models|stellar
M/L]], which is degenerate with the stellar IMF.

## What it is

- Macroimage flux variations on month–year timescales tied to source
  size and stellar Einstein radii (Mediavilla, Pooley, Schechter
  lines of work).
- Single-epoch flux-ratio anomalies in spectra also encode microlensing
  (chromatic vs. achromatic, [[flux-ratio-anomalies|distinct from DM
  anomalies]]).
- Time-monitoring + spectroscopy → ⟨M/L⟩ in the imaged annulus.

## Why it matters

- Independent of dynamical / stellar-population methods for the IMF.
- Complementary to [[bulge-halo-decomposition|lensing + dynamics]]
  bulge-halo decomposition; combined they break the stellar-mass /
  dark-matter degeneracy.
