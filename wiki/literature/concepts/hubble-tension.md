# The Hubble tension

## TL;DR

Local measurements of H0 (distance ladder, Cepheid + SN Ia) give ~73
km/s/Mpc; CMB Planck inference under ΛCDM gives ~67.5 km/s/Mpc; the
mismatch is the "Hubble tension". Strong-lensing time delays are an
independent route.

## What lensing contributes

- [[h0licow]] (6 lenses, 2019): H0 ≈ 73.3 km/s/Mpc, agreeing with the
  distance ladder.
- [[tdcosmo]] (2020) with relaxed mass-model assumptions: H0 ≈ 67–74
  km/s/Mpc, broader uncertainty, consistent with both Planck and the
  distance ladder.
- The shift between these two results is mass-model assumption, not
  data — emphasising that the result depends on what you allow in the
  [[mass-models|mass model]] and the
  [[mass-sheet-degeneracy|MSD]] treatment.

## Outlook

- LSST: ~1000 well-monitored quad lenses.
- Roman + Euclid: ~10⁴ candidates.
- Forecast precision <1% if MSD systematics are controlled.
