# Sources: forecasts and photometric-calibration requirements

The pre-Euclid forecast literature set the photometric and photo-z requirements the
mission's calibration chain ([[zero-point-corrections]], [[euclid-photo-z]]) is built
to meet.

## Ma, Hu & Huterer 2006

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Ma2006`
**Reference:** ApJ 636, 21; arXiv:astro-ph/0506614; doi:10.1086/497068
**Concepts:** [[euclid-photo-z]]

**Supports:**
- Photometric-redshift uncertainties (bias and scatter) propagate directly into
  weak-lensing tomography; sub-percent photo-z calibration is required for
  dark-energy surveys.

## Huterer et al. 2006

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Huterer2006`
**Reference:** MNRAS 366, 101; arXiv:astro-ph/0506030; doi:10.1111/j.1365-2966.2005.09782.x
**Concepts:** [[euclid-photo-z]]

**Supports:**
- Systematic-error requirements for future weak-lensing surveys and prospects for
  self-calibration.

## Amara & Réfrégier 2007

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `AmaraRefregier2007`
**Reference:** MNRAS 381, 1018; arXiv:astro-ph/0610127; doi:10.1111/j.1365-2966.2007.12271.x
**Concepts:** [[euclid-wide-survey]]

**Supports:**
- Optimal survey design for weak-lensing tomography — the area/depth trade
  underlying wide-survey designs like Euclid's.

## Kitching, Taylor & Heavens 2008

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Kitching2008`
**Reference:** MNRAS 389, 173; arXiv:0801.3270; doi:10.1111/j.1365-2966.2008.13419.x
**Concepts:** [[euclid-photo-z]]

**Supports:**
- Systematic effects on dark-energy constraints from 3D weak shear, including
  photometric-redshift systematics.

**Do not use for:** strong-lensing forecasts — for the ~10⁵ lens prediction cite the
mission papers and Collett 2015 (general wiki, [[lens-statistics]]).
